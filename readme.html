🎯 What is This Tool?

This is an interactive backtesting tool that lets you test Bollinger Bands trading strategies using real historical cryptocurrency price data. It simulates what would have happened if you had traded using specific parameters over the past ~42 days.

Purpose: Learn how different strategy settings affect trading performance without risking real money. Understand the mechanics of algorithmic trading, leverage, and risk management.

📈 What are Bollinger Bands?

Bollinger Bands are a technical analysis tool created by John Bollinger. They consist of three lines:

  • •
    Middle Band (SMA): Simple Moving Average of price over N periods (default: 20 hours)
  • •
    Lower Band: SMA minus (Standard Deviation × 2)
  • •
    Upper Band: SMA plus (Standard Deviation × 2)

The Strategy: When price touches or crosses the lower band, it's considered "oversold" (potential buy signal). When price touches the upper band, it's "overbought" (potential sell signal). The bands expand during volatile periods and contract during calm periods.

⚙️ Strategy Parameters Explained

Period (10-30)

Number of hours used to calculate the moving average and standard deviation.

  • • Lower (10-15): More sensitive, reacts quickly to price changes, more signals
  • • Higher (25-30): More stable, filters out noise, fewer but potentially stronger signals
  • • Recommended: Start with 20 (standard default)

Standard Deviation (1.5-3.0)

Controls the width of the bands.

  • • Lower (1.5): Tighter bands, more signals, price touches bands more often
  • • Higher (3.0): Wider bands, fewer signals, only extreme price movements trigger
  • • Recommended: 2.0 (standard default)

Buy Zone (3-10%)

Position within the bands that triggers a BUY signal (from bottom).

  • • 5%: Wait for strong oversold signal (price very close to lower band)
  • • 10%: Enter earlier when price approaches lower band
  • • Recommended: 5% for quality signals

Sell Zone (3-10%)

Position within the bands that triggers a SELL signal (from top).

  • • 5%: Wait for strong overbought signal (95-100% range)
  • • 10%: Exit earlier (90-100% range)
  • • Recommended: 5% for quality exits

Stop Loss (-10% to -30%)

Automatically exits a trade if it loses this percentage (before leverage).

  • • -10%: Tight stop, cuts losses quickly but may exit good trades early
  • • -20%: Balanced protection
  • • -30%: Loose stop, gives trades room but risks larger losses
  • • Recommended: Use -15% to -20% for balance

Position Size (10-100%)

Percentage of available capital to use per trade.

  • • 10-25%: Conservative, allows multiple positions and better risk management
  • • 50%: Moderate exposure
  • • 100%: All-in on every trade (very risky, not recommended for real trading)
  • • Recommended: 20-25% for realistic testing

Trading Fees (0-0.2%)

Fee charged on both entry and exit of each trade.

  • • 0.05%: Low fees (maker rebates on some exchanges)
  • • 0.1%: Standard exchange fees (most realistic)
  • • 0.2%: Higher fees (some exchanges or during high volatility)
  • • Recommended: 0.1% for realistic simulation

📊 Understanding the Metrics

Total Return

Overall percentage gain or loss on your starting capital.

Example: Start with $10,000, end with $12,000 = +20% return

Win Rate

Percentage of trades that were profitable.

Note: A 40% win rate can still be profitable if wins are larger than losses!

Max Drawdown

Largest peak-to-trough decline in account value.

  • • -10% to -20%: Acceptable for most strategies
  • • -20% to -40%: Significant risk, stressful to trade
  • • -40%+: Very high risk, hard to recover from

Profit Factor

Total winnings divided by total losses.

  • • <1.0: Losing strategy (losses exceed wins)
  • • 1.0-1.5: Marginally profitable
  • • 1.5-2.0: Good strategy
  • • 2.0+: Excellent strategy

Sharpe Ratio

Risk-adjusted return measure (higher is better).

  • • <0: Strategy loses money
  • • 0-1: Returns don't justify the risk
  • • 1-2: Good risk-adjusted returns
  • • 2+: Excellent risk-adjusted returns

Liquidation (💀)

When your leveraged position loses 90% or more, exchanges automatically close it to protect their funds.

Example: With 10x leverage, a 9% price drop against you = liquidation

💡 Tips for Effective Backtesting

1️⃣

Start Conservative

Begin with low leverage (5x), stop losses enabled, and 25% position sizing to understand the strategy.

2️⃣

Test Multiple Time Periods

A strategy that works in 7 days might fail over 30 days. Test all date ranges to see consistency.

3️⃣

Watch the Equity Curve

A smooth upward curve is better than wild swings, even if final returns are similar.

4️⃣

Consider All Metrics Together

High returns with huge drawdowns are risky. Look for balance: decent returns + low drawdown + good Sharpe ratio.

5️⃣

Understand Market Conditions

Trending markets and ranging markets require different strategies. Bollinger Bands work best in ranging conditions.

6️⃣

Include Realistic Fees

Set trading fees to 0.1% to see the real impact of costs on your strategy.

7️⃣

Don't Over-Optimize

If you tweak parameters to get perfect historical results, it probably won't work in the future (this is called "overfitting").

⚠️ Important Limitations

🚫 No Slippage

Real trades don't execute at exact prices, especially during volatile movements. This backtest assumes perfect fills.

🚫 No Emotions

Backtests don't include fear, greed, panic, or the stress of watching real money move. Emotions affect real trading significantly.

🚫 Perfect Hindsight

You're seeing what already happened. Real trading involves uncertainty and incomplete information.

🚫 No Market Impact

Large orders move markets. This backtest assumes your trades don't affect prices.

🚫 Limited History

~42 days is not enough to evaluate a strategy fully. You need months or years of data across different market conditions.

🚫 No Black Swan Events

Unexpected crashes, exchange hacks, or regulatory changes can't be predicted or backtested.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Why do some strategies show liquidation?

High leverage amplifies losses. With 40x leverage, a 2.5% move against you results in liquidation. This is realistic behavior in leveraged trading.

Q: Can I use these settings for real trading?

This tool is educational only. Real trading requires more sophisticated analysis, risk management, and understanding of market conditions. Consider it a learning tool, not trading advice.

Q: Why do results change when I select different date ranges?

Different time periods have different market conditions (trending vs ranging, high vs low volatility). A robust strategy should perform reasonably well across multiple periods.

Q: What's a good win rate?

Win rate alone doesn't matter. You can be profitable with 30% win rate if your winners are much larger than your losers. Focus on profit factor and Sharpe ratio instead.

Q: Should I use 100% position sizing?

No. Real traders use 10-25% position sizing for better risk management. 100% position sizing is unrealistic and prevents you from taking multiple positions.

Q: Where does the data come from?

Real historical price data from Binance (primary) and Kraken (fallback). The data shows actual hourly closing prices for the selected cryptocurrency.

Q: What's the best leverage to use?

Lower is safer. 5-10x gives you amplified gains without excessive liquidation risk. Higher leverage (20x+) is extremely risky and mainly used by experienced traders for short-term positions.

Q: Why are there so many signals on the chart?

The tool shows ALL signal levels (Strong Buy, Buy, Consider Buy, etc.). Use the toggles to show only strong signals, or adjust your Buy/Sell Zone parameters to be more selective.

Q: Can this strategy work in any market?

Bollinger Bands work best in ranging/sideways markets. In strong trending markets, the strategy may repeatedly buy dips in a downtrend or sell rallies in an uptrend, leading to losses.

🔬 Advanced Concepts

Mean Reversion Strategy

Bollinger Bands are based on "mean reversion" - the theory that prices tend to return to their average after extreme movements. When price touches the lower band (oversold), it's expected to bounce back up. When it touches the upper band (overbought), it's expected to come back down.

Best for: Ranging markets, consolidation periods, stable volatility

Volatility Indicators

The width of the Bollinger Bands indicates market volatility. Wide bands = high volatility, narrow bands = low volatility. Many traders look for "squeezes" (very narrow bands) as they often precede large moves.

Pro tip: Adjust Std Dev to see how band width affects signal frequency

Risk of Ruin

With high leverage, a series of losses can wipe out your account (liquidation). The probability of this happening increases with: higher leverage, no stop losses, 100% position sizing, and volatile markets.

Mitigation: Use lower leverage, enable stop losses, reduce position size

Compounding Effects

Notice how consecutive wins grow your account exponentially, but consecutive losses shrink it fast. This is compounding. A 50% gain followed by a 50% loss doesn't break even - you're down 25%.

Math: $10,000 → +50% = $15,000 → -50% = $7,500 (net -25%)

Parameter Sensitivity

Small changes in parameters can dramatically affect results. A strategy that's highly sensitive to parameter tweaks is likely "overfit" to historical data and won't work well in the future.

Test: Try Period = 19 vs 20 vs 21. If results change drastically, be skeptical
